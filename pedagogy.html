Our Pedagogy

Learners Valley is backed by a team of curriculum specialists who are former MOE teachers with rich classroom teaching and PSLE marking experiences. Each and every lesson conducted in our classroom is carefully curated and thoroughly thought through based on widely-accepted learning theories and past experiences of enactment in schools. All of our lessons follow a cycle as illustrated below.

5 Key Stages of Our Lesson Cycle

  1. Activate prior knowledge
  2. Explicit teaching
  3. Formative assessment
  4. Consolidation and practice
  5. Feedback and remediation

Our learning resources - notes, worksheets, tests and teaching aids, are all meticulously crafted to go hand in hand with the lessons and the tutor’s guidance. We put in relentless effort to hone our craft of teaching and to perfect every little detail because we want our students to master the syllabus content fully and to achieve a deep level of understanding. Not only do we want them to establish a strong foundation, but also to be able to apply their knowledge confidently.

We acknowledge that nothing is perfect and there will always be room for improvement. Therefore, we continuously review our lesson plans and materials for fine-tuning and to address the ever-changing assessment trends.

5 Key Instructional Strategies

At Learners Valley, we ditched the traditional drill-and-practice method as it makes learning mundane and prohibits learners from achieving a deep level of understanding for application of knowledge. Instead, we employ five instructional strategies that are proven to better help students achieve the learning outcomes.

Inquiry-based learning

Rote memorisation of facts, which is a very common study technique, promotes short-term memory and restricts students from thinking out of the box. Knowledge is best acquired and retained by students when they are able to relate it to the real world. At Learners Valley, we adopt the inquiry-based learning approach to trigger our students’ curiosity to find out facts and to help them develop critical thinking skills.

Diagnosis and Formative Assessment

Is the child ready to learn a new topic? Does he or she have the prerequisite knowledge necessary for learning the new concept? A child struggles to learn not because the content is difficult, but because he or she has yet to get the basics right. At Learners Valley, we conduct a diagnostic test before the start of a new topic. The results of the test informs the tutor about the child’s readiness for the next level of learning. The tutor can then help the child to close the learning gaps first. This ensures that the child is ready to proceed and prevents accumulation of misconceptions.

Feedback and Remediation

A child has done the work. So, what comes next? There is no meaning in doing worksheets after worksheets when the child is not going to revisit and improve on the work done. At Learners Valley, our tutors give quality feedback to the students to help them identify their strengths and weaknesses. The students can then work towards overcoming those weaknesses and develop mastery of the content.

E-pedagogy

Modern technology has reshaped our education landscape by creating endless learning possibilities. At Learners Valley, we integrate technology into our classroom to engage our students and to deepen their learning. We want to encourage active participation from our students by catering to the different learning needs of our students with the endless possibilities supported by technology.

Differentiated Instruction

Every child is unique. No two children learn and grow in the same way and at the same pace. At Learners Valley, we acknowledge this fact and make use of differentiated instruction in our classroom of diverse learners to help each and every child to develop to their fullest potential in the way that is best suited for him or her. We do this by tailoring our teaching strategies and materials to meet each child’s unique interests and needs.
